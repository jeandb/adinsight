# Copy Reviewer

## Purpose

Evaluate and improve advertising copy for Prof Jaque Mendes's ecosystem —
ensuring the copy communicates value clearly, connects emotionally with
teachers, and drives action, within the brand's voice guidelines.

---

## Anatomy of Good Copy for the Pedagogical Niche

### Headline (Main Title)
The headline is the most critical element — it determines whether the user
stops scrolling or keeps going.

**Formulas that work for this audience:**
- **Pain + Solution:** "Cansada de criar atividades do zero? Baixe prontas agora"
- **Specific benefit + Audience:** "60 atividades para o 1º ano — prontas para imprimir"
- **Transformation:** "De professora sobrecarregada a professora organizada"
- **Curiosity with context:** "O que as professoras mais organizadas fazem diferente"
- **Social proof + niche:** "Mais de 50 mil professoras já usam esses recursos"

**Avoid:**
- Generic headlines that could belong to any niche ("Transforme sua vida")
- Exaggerated or unprovable claims ("O melhor recurso do Brasil")
- Headlines that are too long (> 10 words lose impact)
- Overly obvious questions ("Você é professora?")

### Ad Body (Primary Text)
Recommended structure for the niche:

```
[Emotional hook — 1 sentence that expands the headline]
[Specific problem the audience recognizes]
[Solution presented with concrete benefit]
[Proof or credibility — number, result, testimonial]
[Clear and urgent CTA]
```

**Length:** Meta and TikTok favor shorter text (up to 125 characters before "see more"). For higher-value products (Clube, Mentoria), longer text can better educate before conversion.

### CTA (Call to Action)
The CTA must:
- Be specific: "Baixe agora" > "Clique aqui"
- Clearly communicate the next step: "Acesse a loja" > "Saiba mais"
- Create a sense of ease: "em 2 cliques", "sem complicação"
- For Clube: include a scarcity element or immediate benefit ("Garanta seu acesso anual")

## Evaluation Criteria

### 1. Value Proposition Clarity
- On a quick read, is it clear WHAT is being offered?
- Is the main benefit specific or generic?
- Can the audience quickly identify if the ad is meant for them?

### 2. Emotional Connection with the Audience
Check for relevant triggers for teachers:
- **Practicality:** "pronto para usar", "sem precisar criar do zero", "economize tempo"
- **Belonging:** "para professoras como você", "feito por quem entende de sala de aula"
- **Recognition:** "você que se dedica tanto merece ter o melhor"
- **Transformation:** "a professora organizada que você quer ser"
- **Fear of missing out:** "outras professoras já estão usando"

### 3. Brand Voice Alignment
The copy must sound like Prof Jaque — check for:
- **Welcoming (Acolhedor):** speaks with the teacher, not at her
- **Practical (Prático):** focused on real solutions, no fluff
- **Inspiring (Inspirador):** celebrates the teacher, doesn't just sell
- **Authentic (Autêntico):** conversational, approachable but not too informal

**Wrong tone red flags:**
- Too corporate or formal ("Adquira já nosso produto")
- Aggressive or excessive pressure ("Última chance! Não perca!")
- Generic with no personality ("Recursos de qualidade para educadores")

### 4. Platform Policy Compliance
Check for prohibited language:

| Platform | Main restrictions |
|---|---|
| Meta | No "you" + reference to personal characteristics (e.g., "Você está com dificuldades financeiras?"), no guaranteed income claims, no discriminatory language |
| Google | Unverifiable superlatives ("o melhor", "o único"), exaggerated claims |
| TikTok | Guaranteed result claims, artificial urgency language |
| Pinterest | Similar to Meta restrictions, attention to health/financial claims |

### 5. CTA Strength
- Is the CTA present and clear?
- Does it communicate the next step?
- Is there unnecessary friction in the requested action?
